# Daxophone Risks

(Consolidated from design.md ## Risks, restated as a standalone V5 baseline
file.)

- Over-tight clamping can crush wood fibers or mute the tongue.
- Under-tight clamping can buzz, slip, or create false measurement results.
- Bowed-edge preparation affects response and player safety — sharp or
  splintered edges are a handling hazard.
- The contact pickup can hide weak acoustic behavior or introduce mechanical
  artifacts if it becomes an undocumented structural brace.
- Dax-block contact may be expressive but hard to document without a
  repeatable measurement protocol.
- No design table, measured reference, or prototype currently exists; every
  dimension in this packet is `pending_measurement` and must not be treated
  as build-ready.
- Remain at L2 until the tongue coupon, clamp test, bowing test, dax-contact
  test, pickup mount test, and safety review in `design.md` Measurement Gates
  produce evidence.
