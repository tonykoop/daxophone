# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, tongue profiles, fret/contact maps, pickup placement coordinates, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic 3/4 product photograph of a finished daxophone prototype as a real physical object: a single shaped hardwood tongue cantilevered from a clean clamp block mounted on a compact wooden base, the free tongue edge lightly prepared for bowing, a separate hand-held dax block with a smooth curved contact face resting beside it on the base, a small reversible contact pickup and a tidy cable strain relief present near the clamp without dominating the silhouette, satin oil finish on warm figured wood, dark matte metal clamp hardware, realistic tabletop scale cues, clean neutral studio background, soft directional studio light, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic performance scene in a small studio workshop: a player drawing a bow along the prepared edge of the clamped wooden tongue while the other hand presses the hand-held dax block farther along the tongue to shape vocal, gliding, slightly unstable tones, a compact amplifier or recording interface nearby connected to the contact pickup, spare wood tongue coupons and clamp test pieces resting on the bench, expressive but careful posture, anatomically plausible hands, warm natural window light, environmental storytelling, shallow depth of field, concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Photorealistic macro close-up of the signature daxophone mechanism: the clamped root of a shaped wooden tongue held firmly by a dark matte pressure block, fine wood grain running lengthwise along the tongue, bow hair just kissing the prepared free edge, the smooth curved face of the hand-held dax block pressing lightly against the tongue surface farther out, a small contact pickup mounted reversibly near the clamp with a clean cable run, raking light revealing grain and surface texture, high detail, no measurement marks.

## Negative / Avoid

No text/logos/watermarks/fake maker marks, no dimension callouts, no guitar strings or fixed fretboard or fret lines, no impossible clamp geometry or pickup wires passing through solid wood, no unsafe splinters; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
